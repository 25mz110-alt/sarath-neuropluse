Understanding ECG Waveforms

The Electrocardiogram (ECG) is a recording of the electrical activity of the heart. Each beat is composed of several distinct waves that correspond to the contraction of different chambers.

P-Wave: Atrial depolarization, where the top chambers of the heart contract.
QRS Complex: Ventricular depolarization, the main contraction that pumps blood.
T-Wave: Ventricular repolarization, where the heart resets for the next beat.
